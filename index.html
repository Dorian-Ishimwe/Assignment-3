Tribute to Gerald Lawson

Famous Quote: "The whole reason I did games was because people said, 'You can't do it.' I'm one of the guys, if you tell me I can't do something, I'll turn around and do it."

EARLY LIFE : growing up, Gerald 'Jerry' Lawson grew up enjoying the many forms of science. his father was a physician, but due to the racial discrimination they faced back then it wasn't possible for him to get a job in his specialized field. So Gerald grew up with science as a passion rather than an actual career, learning engineering and ham radios and using those skills to fix people's television sets. at 13 he would end up building his own ham radio in his room over the course of a couple of months. He would get the chance to go to 2 colleges in order to learn electronics, but he did not complete neither.

Achievements

  • Created the first home video game console named the Fairchild channel F
  • Created interchangable video game cardtridges
  • Made a Ham (Ameteur) Radio at the young age of 13
  • First Black American engineer during the 'dawn of gaming' era
  • worked at Fairchild Semiconductor as director of engineer and marketing
  • Created 'Demolition Derby', one of the first coin operating machines
